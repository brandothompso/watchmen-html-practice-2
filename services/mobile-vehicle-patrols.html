GPS-Verified Daily Activity Reports, scheduled and randomized patrol routes, and disciplined after-hours coverage for multifamily, hospitality, parking, mixed commercial, and development properties throughout Charlotte.

What Mobile Vehicle Patrols Actually Do

Mobile vehicle patrols are built around visible deterrence and consistent site observation. Marked units circulate parking areas, perimeter lanes, gate points, and shared access zones to keep after-hours property operations monitored and documented.

On-Site Patrol Operations

  • Marked patrol visibility across entries, parking lots, and perimeter approaches.
  • Amenity and common-area drive-through checks during vulnerable time windows.
  • Lighting and gate observations to identify outages, access failures, or exposure points.
  • Randomized route timing layered onto scheduled patrol windows for less predictability.
  • Calm incident observation and immediate dispatch communication when escalation is needed.

Documentation & Accountability

Each shift is anchored by GPS-Verified Daily Activity Reports that provide management with clear, end-of-shift visibility into patrol coverage, site observations, and incident handling. Documentation is practical, field-driven, and built for operational follow-through.

  • GPS checkpoint timestamps tied to patrol route movement and coverage timing.
  • Photo verification attached to relevant findings, conditions, and incident scenes.
  • Incident documentation with location, time, and observed conditions for management review.
  • End-of-shift reporting delivery with concise communication to property leadership.
  • Dispatch-backed operations with supervisor escalation when incidents require it.
