Industry Operational Challenges

Construction sites have moving perimeters, changing subcontractor schedules, and high-value equipment exposure. Patrol operations must adapt to project phases without losing consistency.

Common Pressure Points

  • After-hours visibility gaps around access points, parking, and shared spaces.
  • Need for reliable documentation when communicating with owners and regional teams.
  • Clear dispatch accountability expectations when situations escalate.

Recommended Patrol / Service Model

Mobile patrols anchor perimeter and gate checks, on-foot patrols validate fence lines and material zones, and on-call response closes the loop when alarms or trespass events occur.

FAQ

Can patrol coverage change as the project phase changes?

Yes. Patrol plans can shift from perimeter-focused rounds in early phases to gate control, material yard checks, and equipment-zone visibility as the site evolves.

How is overnight activity documented for stakeholders?

Logs can document gate status, contractor vehicle activity, staging-area observations, and escalation notes that project managers can share with ownership or insurers.
