Dispatch and Accountability Workflow

Response records are built for management review: what triggered the call, what was verified onsite, and what follow-up is required.

  • Dispatch ticket intake with site-specific response instructions.
  • Arrival verification and condition checks logged with timestamps.
  • Management communication notes showing updates and notification points.
  • Closeout summaries with pending items and accountability owner.

Escalation Process

  1. Trigger: alarm signal, camera alert, dispatch call, or property-manager request is received.
  2. Triage: event urgency, site context, and instructions are confirmed before deployment.
  3. Field Verification: responder checks the location, documents conditions, and stabilizes the situation when possible.
  4. Escalation: property contacts and, when required, emergency services are notified through defined communication paths.
  5. Closure: incident timeline, actions taken, and follow-up requirements are documented for management.
